The Problem

The individual does not use his information and processing to grapple directly with complex situations. The situation is generally too complex to yield directly to his motor actions.

The Aborigine Paradox: An aborigine with all our basic sensory-mental-motor capabilities, but without our background of indirect knowledge, cannot organize the proper actions to drive a car, request a library book, or compose a letter.

The Solution

Our culture has evolved means to organize the little things we can do with our basic capabilities so that we can derive comprehension from truly complex situations and accomplish problem-solving.

The New System: A trained human being together with his artifacts, language, and methodology—integrated with computers and computer-controlled information devices.
